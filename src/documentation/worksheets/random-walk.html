Worksheet - Random Walk

If you have never programmed before, this worksheet is a great way to start. And if you have already done some programming you might still find this exercise interesting.

Our objective is to investigate the mathematical idea of a 'random walk'. Imagine that you are standing in the centre of a circle with a radius of 10 paces. But instead of walking straight to the edge, with each pace you make a random turn - of up to to 90 degrees left or right. How many paces will it take to reach the edge? If the turning is truely random then each attempt will take a different number: for example, the picture below shows three such possible random-walk journeys:

But can we determine the average number of paces to reach the edge? Yes, if we could perform the experiment many times. And it turns out that being able to predict the average length of a different kinds of random walk has very significant applications in many different fields of study: physics, biology, ecology, economics ..., even computer science. (You may read more about this on Wikipedia).

So we are going to build just such a simulation.
